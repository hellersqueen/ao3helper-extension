Bye, Bye Miss American Pie

arcane_avenger

Summary:

Natasha Romanoff is faced with her greatest battle yet, cancer. Unable to fight it alone, she grows closer with Bucky.

Notes:

This fic deals with a cancer diagnosis. TW: medical content, chronic illness. Take care of yourselves. ❤

Chapter Text

She’d noticed the signs a few weeks ago. A few small lumps and a distant pain. She was sure it was nothing - things change as you age. Then she started feeling sick, and she began to be too tired to simply get out of bed. The pain increased, so much so that it began to hurt to simply wear her bra, and more lumps had appeared on her breasts. She bit the bullet and pulled a baggy hoody over her bare chest, pain shooting through her as her body grazed the fabric. She took the elevator to 4 floors below her room, where Bruce’s office and the medical bay were situated. Bruce’s door was open and she entered the office, leaning against the wall as Bruce looked up from his computer.

“Morning, Nat. Can I help you?” He asked with a gentle smile, his fingers still tapping away on the keyboard.

“I have a.. problem. I’m sure it’s nothing, but I thought it best to get it checked out.” She kept her gaze locked on the floor below her.

Bruce frowned. “What is it?”

“My breasts,” she replied before a beat had passed. “They hurt constantly and I’ve noticed some lumps appearing.”

Bruce nodded slowly. “Sounds like you need a check up. Would you prefer to have it done by a female doctor?”

Natasha shrugged. “Whatever will make it be done quicker,” she said, finally looking up.

Bruce guided her into one of the medical rooms and instructed her to take a seat then reached for her chart from one of the cupboards. After a quick glance he pulled on some gloves and cleared his throat.

“Can you remove your hoody for me, please?” Bruce asked, moving closer.

Natasha obliged, placing the grey top next to her and shivering slightly as the cold air hit her bare skin.

Bruce’s hand cupped her breasts as he inspected the numerous lumps scattered across her chest. She winced as his hand moved slightly, the pain ever-increasing.

“Have you had any more symptoms?” Bruce asked, removing his gloves and documenting the size and location of the lumps.

“Uh,” Natasha pulled on her hoody. “Nausea, fatigue, I guess.”

Bruce let out a sigh. “I think it’s best if you have a mammogram - essentially a breast X-Ray. It’ll be performed by one of my colleagues, Dr Cho. She’s here at the minute, if you want to get it done with?”

Natasha stood up and nodded, walking with Bruce as he guided her to a separate room. He left her with Helen, who talked her through the procedure, took a blood sample, and before she knew it, it was done with.

She retreated to her room, her head pounding as her body ached for a lie down. She stifled a yawn and collapsed onto her bed, pulling her thick duvet over her body as a wave of nausea hit her.

Dr Cho had assured her she'd get the results to Bruce within a day - being an Avenger had many perks[1].

“Mr. Stark would like me to alert you that dinner is ready,” Friday said, the AIs voice echoing through her room hours later.

Letting out a groan, Natasha forced herself out of bed and changed into some more suitable clothes - skin-tight jeans, a black t-shirt and a leather jacket. The group dinners were hosted on the floor above their rooms, and one below Stark's private floor. Rather than waiting for the elevator, she dashed up the stairs and was greeted by the loud voices of the rest of the Avengers chatting at the table. She took her seat between Clint and Bucky and glanced around, noticing Bruce wasn’t present. Pepper too wasn’t there, but she was on one of her business trips in Tokyo.

Tonight was Friday - pizza night. When Steve had the time, he’d make the team their own pizzas from scratch, but when the team had been swamped with missions and Steve just wanted to relax, they’d order them from their local fine dining restaurant. 20 pizzas, 15 boxes of fries, 15 salads and 30 gooey chocolate cookies. Enough to feed a village, but it would all be gone within an hour.

“Tuck in!” Tony exclaimed from the chair at the end of the table, clasping his hands together.

“Shouldn’t we wait for Bruce?” Steve asked as the others began to fill up their plates.

Tony shook his head. “He’s not joining us tonight, you know how busy he gets.”

Steve nodded, thinking nothing more of it.

Natasha, like the rest of the Avengers, loaded her plate up with food and tucked in. It wasn’t long until the only food left were crumbs in the pizza boxes and Nat felt as if she were ready for a nap. She stood up, keeping a strong grip on the table as a wave of dizziness hit her suddenly.

“I’m off. I need an early night for once. I’ll leave you boys to it,” she said with a small smile and walked back upstairs to her room, resisting the urge to collapse onto her bed.

She peeled off her clothes and changed into some soft pyjamas - black silk bottoms with a matching top. After brushing her teeth, she finally gave into her fatigue and crawled into bed, falling asleep only moments later.

She was awoken at 1am by a knock on her door. Groaning and rubbing her eyes, Nat forced herself upright and paused, listening to see if someone was still outside. Then the knock came again.

With a frown, she climbed out of bed and opened her door.

“Can I come in?” Bruce asked quietly.

She nodded, beckoning him inside and closing the door behind him. He perched on the end of her bed, keeping his hands clasped together.

Natasha flicked on her light and sat down next to him. “Bruce, it’s the middle of the night. You should be in bed.” Her eyes flickered across his face, his eyes red and swollen.

“It couldn’t wait,” He replied, taking a deep breath. “Natasha, you have breast cancer.”

Cancer. The word echoed through Natasha's head. Was this some kind of twisted karma for her actions whilst she was in the red room? Her punishment for being an assassin?

Natasha took a deep breath and tried her best to smile. "I appreciate you telling me, Bruce. Good night." She stood up and opened the door for him.

Bruce, slightly taken aback by her calm manner, left the room with a small sigh. “Come to my office tomorrow, Dr Cho can talk you through what happens next.”

Nat closed the door on him without replying and flicked off the lights. Leaning against the door, she slid to the floor and broke down. She pulled her knees to her head, allowing herself to sob for the first time in a long time. Trying to control her shaky breaths, she ran her hands through her disheveled hair. A gurgle in her stomach made her dash to the bathroom, throwing the lid up just in time for her to give in to the wave of nausea that had hit her for the seemingly millionth time. She heaved up everything she’d consumed that day until all that was left was watery bile.

Groaning, she flushed the toilet and forced herself upright. Is that what her life would be like from now on? Fighting cancer rather than super villains?

Natasha splashed her pale face with cold water before returning to her bed. She was still exhausted, but unable to sleep. Would she still be able to go on missions? How would the other Avengers react? Bruce’s sympathetic tone made her want to punch something, there was no way she could deal with it from everyone else.

Clint. Her best friend. She knew he’d try to act like he was fine, like he wasn’t worried about her, but they were close. Closer to each other than the others. He’d be scared of losing her. At least he’d still have Bucky.

Chapter 2: Chapter 2

Summary:

Natasha meets with Dr. Helen Cho and has a tiring training session.

Chapter Text

The rest of the night passed painfully slowly, and before she’d been able to fall asleep, the rising sun crept between the gap in her curtains. She stared at the dark bags underneath her eyes in the mirror as she got ready, a painful reminder of last night's events.

Begrudgingly, she trudged to Bruce’s office, which she found to be empty.

“Friday, where’s Bruce?” She asked the AI.

“Dr Banner is asleep in his bedroom. Would you like me to wake him up?” The AI replied.

“No, never mind,” she replied with a sigh. As she turned on her heel to leave the room, she was met with Dr Cho standing in the doorway.

“Good morning, Natasha. It’s good to see you. How are you feeling?” Helen entered the room and sat down in Bruce’s chair, pointing for Natasha to sit in the seat opposite.

“Fine,” Nat replied coolly, sliding into the black leather chair.

“Let’s start then, shall we?” She asked with a smile. “First things first, we’ll need to do a CT scan to check how far your cancer has spread. That’ll help us assess what stage you have and what treatment is needed. How does that sound?”

Natasha nodded. “Let’s get it done with.”

Helen guided Nat to the CT room and made her remove any metal she was wearing. She drank a small amount of contrast medium to help make the cancer appear clearer in the scans, then settled into the scanner. Once the scan was over, Helen told Natasha that she would find her later to discuss her findings.

Today was a training day. Natasha changed into her matching red tracksuit and forced down a granola bar, then took the elevator to the training floor. It wasn’t long before the team were (nearly) all gathered in a circle, Steve briefing them on their plan for the session.

“We’ll be focusing on hand-to-hand combat today, getting close up and learning not to rely on our suits or weapons. Some of us will find it easier,” he said, glancing at Bucky and his metal arm. “Others will find it tougher. We don’t all have superserum in our blood.”

Clint scoffed. “No fair. Give me my bow and arrow, and I'll take you down, America's ass.”

“That’s the point, Clint,” Steve raised his eyebrows. “There’ll come a time when you’ll only be able to rely on your body. You need to be strong enough to protect yourself and any civilians around you.”

Clint rolled his eyes and heaved a loud sigh.

“Tony, you can go with Clint. Sam and Bucky pair up, and I’ll go with Natasha.”

Steve led Natasha to the far right of the room, and the pair positioned themselves on the mats with their guards up.

“Ready?” Steve asked to the room. “Go.”

Natasha threw the first punch, but Steve dodged it and threw one in retaliation. Uppercut, jab, hook. Natasha avoided them all.

“You need to work on your aim,” she said with a smirk.

“Uh-huh,” he replied, keeping his eyes fixed on her arms.

He saw it too late when she did a turning kick, landing her foot into his ribcage. He groaned in pain, trying to catch his breath.

“Good move,” he said in between his shaky breaths. “Let’s go again.”

The following round went on for longer, both Avengers being able to anticipate each other's every move. She dodged his punches, moving her body as elegantly as a ballerina, and he avoided her kicks. After 15 minutes of continuous fighting, Natasha began to feel herself slow down. Her kicks got weaker, and she found herself struggling to avoid Steve's attacks. Her stomach churned, and her head started to spin.

She tried a half-hearted turn kick, but he swiftly moved out of the way, causing her to wobble slightly. Steve took advantage of this and threw a series of punches towards her. 2 jabs, then a hook, then another jab. Natasha was unable to avoid them, and instead groaned in pain and stepped backwards with a sigh.

“Fine, you win, whatever,” she said, trying to catch her breath.

“The best man always wins,” he replied with a smirk.

Natasha trudged to the side of the room, letting herself fall onto one of the benches. She grabbed a bottle of water and took a few sips, then realised she was going to struggle to keep it down. She rested her head against the wall behind her and focused on her breathing. In and out, in and out. The other pairs slowly began to finish their fights, and eventually, Bucky came and sat down next to her.

“You okay?” he asked with a frown.

She nodded slightly, the movement making her even more dizzy.

“Are you ill?” he asked.

“I’m fine,” she grumbled.

Bucky sighed and accepted defeat. He grabbed a banana from the snack box and offered one to Natasha, but she shook her head.

“You need to eat, Nat, you look tired.”

“I’m going for a shower,” Natasha said, pushing herself up from the bench and using all her strength to remain upright, then walking to the elevator and retreating to her bedroom. She collapsed onto her bed and, even though she was still wearing her sweaty training clothes, she crawled under her duvet and swiftly fell asleep.

Natasha awoke a few hours later to a gentle knock on the door.

“One minute!” she shouted, jumping out of bed and changing into a fresh pair of clothes from her wardrobe. She opened her door with a small smile.

“Can I come in?” Bucky asked.

“Sure,” Natasha replied, opening the door wider and closing it once he’d entered and taken a seat on her bed. Natasha sat down next to him and tapped her fingers against her thighs.

“Um, I just wanted to check if you were okay. You just seemed a bit, um, ill,” Bucky said, trying not to sound intrusive.

“I’m fine, Buck,” she said reassuringly.

“Good, well, I just thought I’d better check.” Bucky cleared his throat and stood back up. “Well, I’d better leave you to it then. Y’know, I’m always here if you, um, need anything.”

“Yeah, I know, Buck,” she said, “Thanks.”

Bucky left her bedroom, closing the door behind him, leaving Natasha alone again. She crawled back into bed and rested her head on her pillow. Maybe she should have been honest with Bucky. She was starting to realise she couldn’t do it alone.
