Bruce nodded slowly. “Sounds like you need a check up. Would you prefer to have it done by a female doctor?”

Natasha shrugged. “Whatever will make it be done quicker,” she said, finally looking up.

She took her seat between Clint and Bucky and glanced around, noticing Bruce wasn’t present. Pepper too wasn’t there, but she was on one of her business trips in Tokyo.

Tonight was Friday - pizza night. When Steve had the time, he’d make the team their own pizzas from scratch, but when the team had been swamped with missions and Steve just wanted to relax, they’d order them from their local fine dining restaurant. 20 pizzas, 15 boxes of fries, 15 salads and 30 gooey chocolate cookies. Enough to feed a village, but it would all be gone within an hour.

“Tuck in!” Tony exclaimed from the chair at the end of the table, clasping his hands together.

“Shouldn’t we wait for Bruce?” Steve asked as the others began to fill up their plates.

Tony shook his head. “He’s not joining us tonight, you know how busy he gets.”

Steve nodded, thinking nothing more of it.

Natasha, like the rest of the Avengers, loaded her plate up with food and tucked in. It wasn’t long until the only food left were crumbs in the pizza boxes and Nat felt as if she were ready for a nap. She stood up, keeping a strong grip on the table as a wave of dizziness hit her suddenly.

“I’m off. I need an early night for once. I’ll leave you boys to it,” she said with a small smile and walked back upstairs to her room, resisting the urge to collapse onto her bed.

She peeled off her clothes and changed into some soft pyjamas - black silk bottoms with a matching top. After brushing her teeth, she finally gave into her fatigue and crawled into bed, falling asleep only moments later.

She was awoken at 1am by a knock on her door. Groaning and rubbing her eyes, Nat forced herself upright and paused, listening to see if someone was still outside. Then the knock came again.

With a frown, she climbed out of bed and opened her door.

“Can I come in?” Bruce asked quietly.

She nodded, beckoning him inside and closing the door behind him. He perched on the end of her bed, keeping his hands clasped together.

Natasha flicked on her light and sat down next to him. “Bruce, it’s the middle of the night. You should be in bed.” Her eyes flickered across his face, his eyes red and swollen.

“It couldn’t wait,” He replied, taking a deep breath. “Natasha, you have breast cancer.”

Cancer. The word echoed through Natasha's head. Was this some kind of twisted karma for her actions whilst she was in the red room? Her punishment for being an assassin?

Natasha took a deep breath and tried her best to smile. “I appreciate you telling me, Bruce. Good night.” She stood up and opened the door for him.

Bruce, slightly taken aback by her calm manner, left the room with a small sigh. “Come to my office tomorrow, Dr Cho can talk you through what happens next.”

Nat closed the door on him without replying and flicked off the lights. Leaning against the door, she slid to the floor and broke down. She pulled her knees to her head, allowing herself to sob for the first time in a long time. Trying to control her shaky breaths, she ran her hands through her disheveled hair. A gurgle in her stomach made her dash to the bathroom, throwing the lid up just in time for her to give in to the wave of nausea that had hit her for the seemingly millionth time. She heaved up everything she’d consumed that day until all that was left was watery bile.

Groaning, she flushed the toilet and forced herself upright. Is that what her life would be like from now on? Fighting cancer rather than super villains?

Natasha splashed her pale face with cold water before returning to her bed. She was still exhausted, but unable to sleep. Would she still be able to go on missions? How would the other Avengers react? Bruce’s sympathetic tone made her want to punch something, there was no way she could deal with it from everyone else.

Clint. Her best friend. She knew he’d try to act like he was fine, like he wasn’t worried about her, but they were close. Closer to each other than the others. He’d be scared of losing her. At least he’d still have Bucky.
